Welcome! I'm a PhD candidate in political methodology and comparative politics in the Department of Government at American University.

Methodologically, I develop spatiotemporal models for conflict prediction and inference, with a particular focus on Bayesian multilevel models and Gaussian process models. I also work on improving the computational efficiency of Bayesian inference for high-dimensional spatial data and developing dimension-reduction strategies for high-dimensional, large-scale datasets.

Substantively, my research lies at the intersection of political economy of development, conflict, and Chinese overseas development finance.
